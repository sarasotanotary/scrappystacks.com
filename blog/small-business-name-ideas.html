50+ Small Business Name Ideas (And How to Actually Pick One)

By the founder of Scrappy Stacks  ·  6 min read

Most "business name ideas" lists are just a wall of words with no explanation of why any of them work. Here's the actual framework, plus real examples, so you can build a name that fits your business instead of picking one off a list because it sounded nice.

The four things a good name actually does

  • It's easy to say out loud. If someone has to spell it for you on the phone, that's friction every single time someone refers you.
  • It hints at what you do, or nothing at all. The middle ground — vague-but-almost-descriptive — is usually the weakest option. Either be clear ("Suncoast Mobile Detailing") or be a clean brand name that means nothing yet ("Ledger & Co.").
  • It's available. Check your state's business name registry and basic social handles before you fall in love with anything.
  • It doesn't box you in. "Sarah's Birthday Cakes" gets awkward the day you start doing wedding cakes too.

A name doesn't need to be clever. It needs to be repeatable — the test isn't "do I love it," it's "would a customer say this correctly to a friend after hearing it once?"

A quick gut-check before you commit

Say the name out loud five times in a row. If it still feels good on the fifth time, and nobody in the room asks you to repeat it, it's probably solid.
